DRAFT LEGAL & CLASSIC SPRINT
MIXED TEAM RELAY

Course Information For Draft Legal & Classic Sprint Individuals

A highly exciting and highly competitive sprint distance race with distances of 750 meters for the swim, 20km for the bike, and 5km for the run. The collegiate women will race in wave 1 (7:30 AM), and the collegiate men will race in the wave 2 (9:00 AM).

Draft Legal Participants:

  • Only 75 men in wave 1 (Men) and only 75 women in wave 2 (Women).
  • Only 5 men/5 women from each collegiate team will be permitted. This restriction will be lifted on August 1, 2016 if there are remaining spots.
  • 35 spots reserved for community members in each draft legal wave.

Classic Participants:

  • Open to community members as well as collegiate athletes.

Rules and Additional Information:

Please read through the "General Info" tab as well as the USAT Draft Legal Rules Summary HERE if you will be partaking in a draft legal event.

SWIM COURSE

Swim course: ~500 meters, 1 lap
The swim is an open water start, with athletes treading water at the start line until the gun goes off. Racers will swim counterclockwise towards the buoys, before heading back towards the dock where they will be pulled out of the water. Wetsuits are recommended.

TRANSITION #1

Transition 1:
Racers will then follow the road to transition, at a grassy field roughly half a lap away.

BIKE COURSE

Bike Course: 20 kilometers, 3 laps
The bike is a fast and flat course (with great drafting capability, ONLY for draft legal). Racers will come out of transition, taking a right onto University Ave, eventually exiting the Berkeley Marina area when turning right onto W. Frontage Rd, which runs parellel to the 580 Highway and the San Francisco Bay Trail. Racers will stay on W. Frontage Rd for a total of 3 laps, each including 2 U-turns, before returning onto University Ave and heading into Transition 2.

TRANSITION #2

Transition 2:
The same location as transition 1, racers will dismount their bikes and start the run portion of the race from here.

RUN COURSE

Run Course: 5 kilometers, 2 laps
The run is a quick and flat course. Runners will return along the same route they started on for that exciting sprint finish and some tasty post-race snacks.


Course Information For Mixed Team Relay

One of the most exciting races to watch is the mixed team relay! The triathlon mixed team relay consists of teams of four, comprised of two men and two women. The women go 1st and 3rd, while the men go 2nd and 4th. Each athlete completes a super-sprint, draft-legal triathlon, before tagging their teammate. The race will be 200 meters for the swim meters for the swim, ~3.5 kilometers for the bike, and ~1 kilometer for the run.

Rules and Additional Draft Legal Information:

Please read through the "General Info" tab as well as the USAT Draft Legal Rules Summary HERE

SWIM COURSE

Swim course: 200 meters (some distance reflected in transition), 1 lap
The swim is an open water start, with athletes treading water at the start line until the gun goes off. Racers will swim counterclockwise towards the buoys, before heading back towards the dock where they will be pulled out of the water.

TRANSITION #1

Transition 1:
Racers will then follow the road to transition, at a grassy field roughly half a lap away.

BIKE COURSE

Bike course: ~3.5 kilometers, 1 lap
The bike course is a quick 2 mile out and back along the bay on Frontage road. It is a fast and flat road, with no climbs and one tight u-turn. Drafting is allowed during this portion of the race.

TRANSITION #2

Transition 2:
The same location as transition 1, racers will dismount their bikes and start the run portion of the race from here.

RUN COURSE

Run course: 1 kilometer, 1 lap
The run course is also a fast and flat out and back, where racers run just over half a mile on the San Francisco Bay trail before turning around. The run finishes at the dock, where racers will run to tag the next person in the relay. The last racer, however, will finish at the same finish as the draft legal race.


Rules

We are very excited to host this draft-legal race as well as classic sprint!

Please review the following information so we can keep this race as safe and enjoyable as possible!

Ways to avoid time penalties:

Swim:

  • Make forward progress once the race start signal has been sounded
  • Do not block or interfere the forward progress of another competitor

T1:

  • Place swim cap, goggles, and wetsuit in the basket
  • Do not touch bike until helmet is on and clipped

Bike:

  • Mount bike after mount line
  • Dismount bike before dismount line
  • Lap out rule will be enforced for Draft Legal events

T2:

  • Re-rack bike either by seat or both brake levers
  • Place helmet and cycling shoes in basket

Run:

  • If race number is on the board at penalty box, stop and serve penalty*
  • the penalty box will be located at aid station 2 for the sprint (~0.75 miles into the run) and at the only aid station on the MTR (at University/Marina, ~0.3 miles into the run)

Penalty/Disqualification During the Race:

  • Failure to comply with the actions listed above will result in a 10 second (15 second for swim) time penalty to be served at the penalty box.
  • Athletes must move forward after the start signal
  • Helmet chin-strap MUST be buckled at all times when touching the bike
  • Failure to serve time penalties will result in disqualification.
  • Blocking or interfering with the forward progress of another competitor is not allowed and may result in disqualification.
  • Unsportsmanlike conduct, including inappropriate language directed at fellow competitors, volunteers, or officials, may result in disqualification

Additional Rules:

  • All items in transition area must be used at some point throughout the race (so towels are not allowed)
  • All handlebars must have bar-end plugs as per usual triathlon regulation.
  • USAT will be checking all bicycles and equipment before allowing participants to enter transition in the morning.
  • Trisuit or 2 Piece Trisuit allowed. Must be covering the torso area.
  • A race number belt is not necessary

Rules and Additional Information for Draft Legal:

  • No Aero Helmets Permitted/Must have individual helmet with chin-strap.
  • All handlebars must be drop-style, no TT bikes allowed.
  • Each participant must have their own bicycle. Bicycles must be triangular frame, with drop-style handlebars. Aerobars must not extend past the brake levers of the bicycle and must be capped and bridged together. Additionally, no bar-end gear shifters allowed.
  • No disk wheels allowed- bikes must have a minimum of 16 spokes
  • If you are lapped out, you are disqualified from the race and you must walk your bike back to transition. Please note, this is for the safety of the racers.
  • Please Read the USAT Draft Legal Rules Summary HERE
